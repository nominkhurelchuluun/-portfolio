📊 Data Science Projects

Here are some of the analytical projects I’ve worked on, combining Python, data visualization, and statistical modeling.

1️⃣ Ordinary Least Squares vs Weighted Least Squares

Overview: Analyzed earthquake data from earthquake_query.csv to identify regions with the highest average magnitudes using Python (Pandas, NumPy).

Analysis: Cleaned missing data, computed top-5 regions with the largest mean magnitudes, and compared estimation accuracy between OLS and WLS regression models.

Key Insight: Weighted Least Squares provided more reliable estimates for regions with high variance in earthquake magnitudes, improving model fit and stability.

2️⃣ Air Quality and Mental Health in Oregon

Overview: Collected and analyzed annual PM2.5 air quality data from the EPA’s AQS API (2009–2022) for five U.S. states, focusing on Oregon.

Analysis: Modeled the relationship between mean_avg PM2.5 levels and mental health indicators to test whether air pollution has a negative psychological impact.

Approach: Used visualization and regression to examine the link, avoiding strong distributional assumptions. Also designed an optimization-based objective function to help parents balance children’s outdoor playtime and air quality exposure in NYC.

Key Insight: Found a weak but consistent negative association between poor air quality and mental well-being — suggesting environmental factors significantly affect health outcomes.
